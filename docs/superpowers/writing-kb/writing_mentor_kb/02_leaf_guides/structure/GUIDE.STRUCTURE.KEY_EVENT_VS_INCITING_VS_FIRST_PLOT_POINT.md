## Purpose
Disambiguate three commonly conflated early structural moments in Weiland's framework.

## Source-aligned notes
Weiland explicitly warns that the Inciting Event is often misidentified, and separately emphasizes that the Key Event should not be confused with the First Plot Point.

## Working distinctions
- Inciting Event: early disturbance that initiates the story's conflict field
- Key Event: the moment that personally hooks the protagonist into the central conflict
- First Plot Point: the threshold-crossing turn that commits the protagonist to the main conflict and launches Act Two
